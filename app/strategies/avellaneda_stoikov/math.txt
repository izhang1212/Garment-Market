
VARIABLES:
- μ (fair value) estimated true price
- σ (volatility) uncertainty / price dispersion
- q (inventory) how many units you hold
- γ (risk aversion) how much you dislike risk
- k (liquidity) how quickly trades happen
- T (time horizon) how long you plan to trade

KEY IDEAS:
Reservation Price: r = μ − qγ(σ^2)T
    - Interpretation: The true price to the trader, not the market
    - Takes inventory (q) into account (i.e. if q > 0, price shifts down, if q < 0, price shifts up)
    - qγ(σ^2)T: inventory penalty term  
        - If T increases (longer horizon), the penalty becomes larger, and r shifts further away from FV
        - If T is small, r ~ μ, so we don't care much about the inventory since we are exiting the market soon

Optimal Spread: γ(σ^2)T + (2/γ) * ​ln(1 + (γ/k)​)
    - γ(σ^2)T: The risk term 
        - higher volatility (σ) or risk aversion (γ) = wider spread
        - Larger time horizon (T) = more risk = wider spread
            - If T is small, we only care about the liquidity term (i.e. how easy it is to exit)
    - (2/γ) * ​ln(1 + (γ/k)​): Liquidity term
        - low liquidity = higher spread, vise versa (harder to exit market)

Bid/Ask Quotes: 
- bid = r - (spread/2)
- ask = r + (spread/2)
    - Centers quotes around reservation price (how much it is worth to us) rather than FV (market)

EXAMPLE:
Given
- μ = 246
- σ = 5
- q = 2
- γ = 0.1
- k = 1.0
- T = 1

Reservation Price: r = 246 − (2 * 0.1 * 25 * 1) 
                     = 246 − 5 
                     = 241
Spread = (0.1 * 25 * 1) + (2/0.1) * ln(1 + 0.1 / 1)
       ~ 4.4
Bid = 241 - 4.4 = 236.6
Ask = 241 + 4.4 = 245.4

================================================================================
EV MODEL
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The EV model is a heuristic approach. It does not derive an optimal spread from
a formula. Instead, it simulates many candidate spreads and picks the one with
the highest expected value. Because the bid and ask are centered around the
reservation price (not fair value), and fill probability is measured from fair
value, the two sides end up at different distances from FV. This means bid_EV
and ask_EV are naturally different — the model implicitly penalizes taking on
more inventory in the direction you are already loaded.


PARAMETERS (inputs we provide)
-----------
- transactions:         historical trade records (price + timestamp)
- decay_lambda (λ):     how fast old transactions lose relevance
- spread_multipliers:   list of candidate spread widths to try (e.g. [1.0, 1.5, 2.0])
- inventory (q):        how many units we currently hold
- inventory_penalty (α): how aggressively to shift quotes for inventory
- aggressiveness:       how steeply fill probability falls as quotes move away from FV
- min_spread:           floor on the spread — never quote tighter than this


VARIABLES (derived from parameters)
---------
Fair Value (FV):
    FV = (∑ w_i * x_i) / ∑ w_i,  where  w_i = e^(-λ * t_i)
    Recency-weighted average of transaction prices. Recent trades carry more weight.

Volatility (σ):
    σ = sqrt( (∑ w_i * (x_i - FV)²) / ∑ w_i )
    Recency-weighted standard deviation around FV. Measures price uncertainty.

Reservation Price (r):
    r = FV - α * q
    Our internal valuation adjusted for inventory. Excess inventory (q > 0)
    lowers r, shifting our quotes downward to encourage selling.


SIMULATING THE BEST BID/ASK
----------------------------
For each candidate spread_multiplier k, we generate a quote pair and score it.
There is no formula that tells us the right k — we try all of them and pick the best.

    spread   = max(k * σ,  min_spread)

    bid      = r - spread / 2
    ask      = r + spread / 2

    Note: bid/ask are symmetric around r, but NOT symmetric around FV when q ≠ 0.
    When long (q > 0), r < FV, so the ask is closer to FV than the bid is.

Fill probability — measured as distance from FV:
    P(fill_bid)  = exp(-aggressiveness * |bid - FV| / σ)
    P(fill_ask)  = exp(-aggressiveness * |ask - FV| / σ)

    A quote closer to FV is more likely to execute. Because ask is closer to FV
    when long, P(fill_ask) > P(fill_bid) — the model is more likely to sell,
    which is exactly the inventory correction we want.

Expected value per side:
    bid_EV   = (FV - bid)  * P(fill_bid)    ← profit per fill × chance of filling
    ask_EV   = (ask - FV)  * P(fill_ask)

    total_EV = bid_EV + ask_EV

The spread_multiplier with the highest total_EV is chosen. The final bid/ask
are the quotes from that winning candidate.




================================================================================
AVELLANEDA-STOIKOV MODEL
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The AS model derives the optimal spread analytically from a continuous-time
stochastic control problem (Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation). Rather than
simulating candidate spreads, it computes exactly what the bid and ask should
be given the model's parameters. The optimizer sweeps over uncertain parameter
values (γ, κ, T) and uses the same EV scoring to find the best combination.


PARAMETERS (inputs we provide)
-----------
- fair_value (s):           estimated true price (typically taken from EV model output)
- volatility (σ):           price uncertainty (typically taken from EV model output)
- inventory (q):            how many units we currently hold
- risk_aversion (γ):        how much we penalize holding inventory risk
- liquidity (κ):            rate at which market orders arrive
- time_horizon (T):         how long we plan to be in the market
- aggressiveness:           fill probability decay rate (shared with EV model)
- min_spread:               floor on the spread


VARIABLES (derived from parameters)
---------
Reservation Price (r):
    r = s - q * γ * σ² * T
    Inventory penalty scales with volatility, risk aversion, and time horizon.
    As T → 0 (end of session), r → s and inventory barely affects the quote.

Optimal Spread (δ):
    δ = γ * σ² * T  +  (2/γ) * ln(1 + γ/κ)

    Two components:
      - Risk term    [γ * σ² * T]:         wider when vol / risk aversion / time is high
      - Liquidity term [(2/γ) * ln(...)]:  wider when κ is low (few arriving orders),
                                            so each fill needs to be more profitable


FORMULAS FOR BID/ASK
--------------------
    bid  = r - δ / 2
    ask  = r + δ / 2

Unlike the EV model, the spread width is not searched — it is computed directly
from the formula above. The quotes are symmetric around the reservation price
because the model assumes buy and sell orders arrive at equal rates.

EV scoring — used by the optimizer to select the best (γ, κ, T) combination:
    P(fill_bid)  = exp(-aggressiveness * |bid - FV| / σ)
    P(fill_ask)  = exp(-aggressiveness * |ask - FV| / σ)
    bid_EV       = (FV - bid)  * P(fill_bid)
    ask_EV       = (ask - FV)  * P(fill_ask)
    total_EV     = bid_EV + ask_EV

The optimizer sweeps all combinations of risk_aversion × liquidity × time_horizon
and returns the candidate with the highest total_EV.




================================================================================
DIFFERENCES
================================================================================

HOW THE SPREAD IS DETERMINED
    EV model:   no formula. Sweeps candidate spread_multipliers, picks best total_EV.
                The spread width emerges from simulation, not theory.
    AS model:   closed-form formula. δ = γσ²T + (2/γ)ln(1 + γ/κ).
                Sweeps uncertain model parameters (γ, κ, T) instead of the spread itself.

QUOTE SYMMETRY
    EV model:   quotes are symmetric around r, but asymmetric relative to FV when
                inventory ≠ 0. The bid and ask have different fill probabilities and
                EVs — this asymmetry is what drives the inventory correction.
    AS model:   also symmetric around r, same consequence relative to FV.
                The δ/2 symmetry follows from assuming equal bid/ask arrival rates.

INVENTORY PENALTY
    EV model:   r = FV - α * q. Flat constant penalty. α has no connection to
                volatility or time — purely a tunable number.
    AS model:   r = s - q * γ * σ² * T. Penalty grows with volatility, risk
                aversion, and time horizon. Grounded in the theory.

FAIR VALUE SOURCE
    EV model:   computes FV internally from transaction history.
    AS model:   takes FV as an input. In this codebase it uses the EV model's output.

WHAT THE OPTIMIZER TUNES
    EV model:   spread_multiplier (1 dimension).
    AS model:   risk_aversion, liquidity, time_horizon (3 dimensions, grid search).
                Both use the same total_EV criterion to pick the winner.

THEORETICAL BASIS
    EV model:   heuristic. The fill probability formula is a reasonable approximation,
                not a derived result. No proof of optimality.
    AS model:   derived from a formal stochastic control problem. Under the model's
                assumptions it produces the mathematically optimal quotes.
